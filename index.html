Why Does SSC Reject Photos? (Avoid These Mistakes)

When applying for SSC exams, the automated verification system scrutinizes every pixel. To ensure your application is accepted, avoid these fatal errors:

  • No DOP (Date of Photo): SSC notifications explicitly state that the date the photograph was taken must be clearly printed. If you fail to edit date in image online properly, rejection is guaranteed.
  • Oversized or Undersized Files: The portal will physically not accept files above 50KB. Conversely, files below 20KB are deemed too low-resolution and are rejected by invigilators.
  • Incorrect Aspect Ratio: Uploading a square selfie or a landscape photo warps the image on the admit card. Our tool enforces a strict portrait aspect ratio.
  • Spectacles and Caps: Do not wear dark glasses, tinted spectacles, or caps in your photograph. Both ears should be clearly visible.
